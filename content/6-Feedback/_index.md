> Here, I would like to share my personal opinions and practical experiences during my internship at First Cloud AI Journey (FCAJ), particularly from the perspective of an intern pursuing the Information Assurance (Pentest/Red Team) path.

### Overall Evaluation

**1. Working Environment** The working environment at FCAJ is highly comfortable and flexible. I was provided with maximum time and space to research vulnerability assessment labs or set up internal networks, as long as it aligned with learning objectives and general organizational rules. Although the team's openness isn't always overtly expressed on the surface, whenever I had technical questions, the members were always happy to provide thorough and enthusiastic explanations.

**2. Support from Mentor / Team Admin** In reality, there isn't anyone on the Admin team specifically pursuing the Pentester or Red Teamer path for me to learn from directly. Therefore, I mostly had to self-teach. However, the Admins possess profound expertise in web design, web development, and Cloud architecture. Interacting with and learning from the perspective of "Builders" inadvertently gave me a much stronger foundation for understanding how to uncover system vulnerabilities.

**3. Relevance of Work to Academic Major** The solid foundational knowledge acquired at university (concerning Networks, Operating Systems, Linux, etc.) served as the most crucial "stepping stones" for me to confidently take my first steps "onto the cloud," thereby building and subsequently attempting to break systems. 

My biggest realization was recognizing the fundamental difference in administrative mindsets: A traditional on-premise system typically focuses on securing physical perimeters and network devices; whereas a cloud-native system revolves around Identity and Access Management (IAM), micro-permissions, and serverless architecture. Even though the core network protocols remain similar, the attack surface and risk management on the Cloud present an entirely different challenge.

**4. Learning & Skill Development Opportunities** Although I had to self-direct my learning in the offensive security domain, being immersed in an environment filled with cutting-edge Cloud technologies helped me hone my self-learning abilities and rapidly adapt to complex system architectures.

**5. Company Culture & Team Spirit** FCAJ's culture emphasizes autonomy. Everyone is focused on their own tasks and goals but is always ready to collaborate when necessary. This aligns perfectly with the personality and the high-concentration nature required in security work.

---

### Additional Questions

* **What did you find most satisfying during your internship?** My most satisfying moments were my actual, practical "wins": I successfully discovered system vulnerabilities on FCAJ's registration/attendance portal, identified configuration flaws within an AWS system, and experienced the thrill of successfully exploiting weaknesses on our group project's web management system.

* **What do you think the company should improve for future interns?** To be honest, I don't have any specific recommendations. I recognize that FCAJ's core orientation leans towards Cloud Building and Operations (Cloud Architecture/Builder) rather than the Offensive Security path. Therefore, FCAJ is already performing excellently in alignment with its organizational mission and goals.

* **If recommending to a friend, would you suggest they intern here? Why or why not?** I would definitely recommend FCAJ to friends wanting to pursue Cloud Computing, DevOps, or defensive Cloud Security, as it is an ideal environment to gain hands-on experience with real systems. However, for those who purely want to start from scratch in Pentest/Red Teaming, I would advise them to be prepared with a very high capacity for self-directed learning if they choose to intern here.
